Networking Opportunities

Why Networking Matters

Networking opens doors to mentorship, referral-based opportunities, and industry knowledge. Good networking accelerates career growth by connecting you with hiring managers, mentors, and project collaborators. A strong network helps you discover hidden job openings, receive timely feedback, and gain real-world insights into company culture and expectations.

Pro tip: Keep a short note after every conversation (topic, follow-up date, and one action). This habit turns casual chats into useful, long-term connections.

Networking Dos & Don'ts

Do

  • Be specific in your ask — e.g., "Can you review my one-page resume?"
  • Follow up within 48 hours with a short thank-you and next step.
  • Offer value: share relevant articles, intros, or feedback.
  • Keep your LinkedIn profile concise and current.

Don't

  • Don't ask for a job on first contact — build rapport first.
  • Avoid mass, impersonal messages — personalize every outreach.
  • Don't forget to track conversations and promised follow-ups.

Quick Tools & Next Steps

  • Prepare a 30-second intro: Who you are, what you do, and the one thing you want.
  • Track contacts: Use a simple spreadsheet with columns: name, platform, topic, follow-up date.
  • Follow-up templates: Keep two short templates — one for first follow-up, one for thank-you after help.
  • Event etiquette: Arrive early, listen actively, and follow up with a short, specific ask.
